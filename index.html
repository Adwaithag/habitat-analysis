LiDAR Forest Structure

Developing a LiDAR-based forest analysis system to detect individual trees, estimate structural variables, and compare forest habitats using ground-truth ecological data from the shola forests of Kodaikanal.

Project Overview

This LiDAR-based forest analysis project focuses on understanding the structural complexity and habitat characteristics of forests in Kodaikanal using handheld LiDAR data and field-based ecological measurements. The study includes 3D point cloud visualization, canopy height analysis, habitat assessment, GBH estimation, and spatial distribution of trees for understanding forest structure and ecological variation.

Key Findings

  • Dense and multi-layered canopy structure observed across Bombay Shola.
  • LiDAR data effectively captured forest structure and habitat complexity in 3D.
  • Open canopy patches and dense vegetation zones were clearly distinguishable.
  • Ground observations supported the accuracy of LiDAR-derived analysis.
  • Spatial distribution analysis revealed variation in tree density across the landscape.

Conclusion

This project demonstrated the usefulness of handheld LiDAR for studying forest structure and habitat characteristics in the shola forests of Kodaikanal. By combining LiDAR data with ecological field observations, the study provided detailed insights into canopy structure, vegetation density, habitat openness, and spatial variation within the forest ecosystem.
