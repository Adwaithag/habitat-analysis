Ecological Analysis

Vegetation Density

Variability in vegetation density was observed across habitat patches, indicating differences in canopy closure and understory structure.

Canopy Structure

Layered canopy structure and light penetration differences suggest ecological heterogeneity within the forest system.

Habitat Openness

Ground-level visibility and canopy openness varied spatially, reflecting microhabitat variation and terrain influence.

Field Observation

Visual field documentation supports interpretation of habitat complexity, vegetation continuity, and ecological gradients.

Key Findings

  • Forest habitat exhibited heterogeneous structural composition.
  • Canopy layering influenced light penetration and understory visibility.
  • Vegetation density varied significantly across observed patches.
  • Habitat complexity suggests diverse ecological microenvironments.
  • Visual observations aligned with expected forest structural variation.

Conclusion

The habitat demonstrates clear structural and ecological variability influenced by vegetation density, canopy arrangement, and terrain conditions. Combined field observations and visual habitat documentation provide a strong foundation for ecological interpretation and future LiDAR-supported forest analysis.
