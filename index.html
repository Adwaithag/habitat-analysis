Project Overview

A LiDAR-based forest analysis project focused on studying the structural characteristics of forests in Kodaikanal using handheld LiDAR data and ground-truth ecological measurements. The project includes 3D point cloud visualization, canopy height model generation, individual tree detection, GBH estimation, and comparative analysis for understanding forest structure and habitat dynamics.

Ecological Analysis

Vegetation Density

The vegetation density analysis helped identify areas with dense canopy cover as well as more open regions within the forests of Kodaikanal. Using handheld LiDAR data, the study provided a detailed view of tree distribution, canopy structure, and overall forest complexity through 3D visualization and canopy height models, helping better understand the variation in vegetation across the landscape.

Canopy Structure

The canopy structure analysis focused on understanding the vertical profile and layering of the forest in Kodaikanal. By using LiDAR-derived canopy height models and 3D point cloud data, the study highlighted differences in tree height, canopy continuity, and structural variation within the forest, providing insights into habitat complexity and forest architecture.

Habitat Openness

The habitat openness analysis helped identify areas with closed canopy cover and more open forest patches within the study area in Kodaikanal. Using LiDAR-derived structural data, the analysis provided insights into light availability, canopy gaps, and spatial variation in forest openness, helping better understand habitat conditions and vegetation patterns across the landscape.

Field Observation

Visual field documentation supports interpretation of habitat complexity, vegetation continuity, and ecological gradients.

Key Findings

  • Dense and multi-layered canopy structure observed across major parts of Bombay Shola
  • LiDAR data effectively captured forest structure and habitat complexity in 3D
  • Open canopy patches and dense vegetation zones were clearly distinguishable
  • Ground observations supported the accuracy of LiDAR-derived forest structural analysis

Conclusion

This project showed how handheld LiDAR can be used as an effective tool to study and visualize forest structure in Kodaikanal. By combining LiDAR data with field observations, it was possible to identify individual trees, estimate tree height and GBH, and better understand variations in vegetation density, canopy structure, and habitat openness within the forest. The 3D analysis provided a clearer picture of how different parts of the forest vary in terms of canopy cover and structural complexity. Overall, the study highlights the usefulness of LiDAR technology for ecological research, habitat assessment, and long-term forest monitoring in complex shola forest ecosystems.
